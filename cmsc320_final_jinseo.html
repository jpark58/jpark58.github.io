Analysis on 15 years of power outages across the United States¶

1. Motivation¶

For the final project, I decided to analyze the power outage cases across the United States and find the relationship between power outages with its casues such as physical attack, storm, etc.

Before I came to the United States, I have never thought about the power outage would be a problem and had no such experience. However, since I came here, I have experienced the power outage every time when the storm hit my area. Also, I realized that the power supply system is not quite stable compared to my home country.

At the end of this project, I expected to find that the storm is the major factor and also find out other factors that affected the power outage. In addition to that, I look forward to finding a good machine learning model to predict the number of affected customers by some factors that we are going to find in this tutorial.

2. Data Collection & Management¶

I found an interesting dataset from Kaggle, https://www.kaggle.com/autunno/15-years-of-power-outages.

This dataset contains outage data from 2000 up until 2014, with the following information.

  • Event Description: Reason of the outage (e.g. Vandalistm, Severe Weather, etc)
  • Year: Year of the outage (e.g. 2014)
  • Month: Month of the outage (e.g. November)
  • Date Event Began: Date of the outage (e.g. 11/13/2017)
  • Time Event Began: Time the outage was registered (e.g. 15:05:00)
  • Date of Restoration: Date the outage was resolved
  • Time of Restoration: Time the outage was resolved
  • Respondent: The company that acted upon the outage
  • Geografic Area: Region of the outage (e.g. Missisippi, Texas, New York, Salt Lake City, etc)
  • NERG Region: (NERC refers to the North American Electricity Reliability Corporation, formed to ensure the reliability of the grid).
  • Demand Loss (MW): How much energy was not transmited/consumed during the outage.
  • Number of customers affected: How many consumers (e.g. homes, offices, industry, etc) were left to their devices.
  • Tags: Summary event description (e.g. wild fire, vandalism, severe weather)

df['Reason'] = ''
for idx, row in df.iterrows():
    word = row['Cause'].lower()
    if ('weather' in word or 'storm' in word or 'cold' in word or 'thunder' in word or 'wind' in word or 'ice' in word or 'lightning' in word or 'snow' in word or 'hurricane' in word or
       'tornado' in word or 'fog' in word or 'severe' in word or 'rain' in word or 'strike' in word or 'ice storm'in word ):   
        df.loc[idx, 'Reason'] = 'Weather'
    elif ('attack' in word or 'physical' in word or 'vandalism' in word or 'theft' in word):
        df.loc[idx, 'Reason'] = 'Attack'
    elif ('fuel' in word or 'failure' in word or 'system' in word or 'supply' in word or 'emergency' in word or 'voltage' in word or 'interruption' in word or
         'shed' in word or 'load' in word):
        df.loc[idx, 'Reason'] = 'System'
    else:
        df.loc[idx, 'Reason'] = 'Others'

5. Conclusion¶

In this tutorial, I analyzed the power outages across the United States from 2000 to 2014. In the initial dataset, the causes of the power outage were described in unorganized strings as many as 323 differnt categories. So, I parsed and categroized them by keywords such as storm, ice, attack, etc. Then, I performed Exploratory Data Analysis tidying data as needed depending on the plot that I wanted to show. With the lineplot, I found that the number of power outage increased until 2011 and drastic fall down in 2012. This result rejected my expectation that the number of the power outage would decrease as time goes. With the barplot, I found that coatal areas tend to have more frequent power outage. For simplicity, I cut the number of cases at 50 and that was enough to show the patterns for near coast areas. With the piechart, we found the portion of each factor of the power outage. As I expected, "Weather" took a significant amount of the pie chart followed by "System", "Attack", and "Others". With the heatmap, I found the correlation between the number of affected by the power outage and each reason factors. Interestingly, "Others" had stronger correlation with the affected than "Attack" did. Lastly, I performed some machine learing and used Linear Regression model to predct the number of affected customers by reason factors. The model has a good accuracy of 0.763 which is close to 1. So, we also found a good model to predict.
